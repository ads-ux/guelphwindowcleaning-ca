Can You Clean Second and Third Storey Windows in Guelph?

Published 2026-04-15 — Guelph Window Cleaning

Yes — cleaning second and third storey windows is routine for professional window cleaning companies in Guelph. Here's how it works and what to expect.

Water-Fed Pole Systems

The standard approach for two-storey Guelph homes is a water-fed pole system (also called a pure water or WFP system). Here's how it works:

  • A telescoping carbon fibre or fibreglass pole reaches 10–15 metres from the ground
  • Water is purified through deionization (removing minerals that cause spotting), then pumped up through the pole
  • A brush on the end scrubs the glass and frame while pure water rinses continuously
  • The window dries spot-free because there are no dissolved minerals to leave residue

This method is safer for the technician (no ladder work on unstable ground), faster per window, and produces excellent results on standard windows.

When Ladders Are Used

Some window configurations require ladder access rather than a water-fed pole:

  • Windows under a covered porch roof that the pole can't reach from an angle
  • Bay or angled windows where the brush can't make proper contact
  • Windows requiring interior cleaning (the pole system only works exterior)
  • Third storey windows beyond pole reach in some configurations

Professional Guelph window cleaners assess each property before quoting — ladder requirements don't typically change the price significantly unless there are unusual access challenges.

Third Storey Windows

Third storey exterior windows are accessible with extended water-fed poles (some reach up to 20+ metres) or via specialized ladder equipment. Most Guelph residential properties have accessible third storey windows through one of these methods. Truly difficult access scenarios (set-back dormers, steep rooflines) may require rope access or additional setup costs — a professional will flag this in the quote.

What You Need to Do

For exterior-only cleaning using the water-fed pole method, you typically don't need to be home — the technician works from the ground or around the perimeter. For interior cleaning, someone must be present. Ensure walkways around the home are clear of obstacles and that outdoor water access (a hose bib) is available and turned on.
